# Hardly Selling Methodology

A complete framework for ethical, high-ticket sales through deep discovery, buyer archetype detection, and structured call flow.

---

## Call Phases

The Hardly Selling methodology structures every call into 13 phases. Timing is a guide, not a straitjacket — the prospect's pace matters more than the clock.

### Phase 1: Open (0-2 min)
Rapport and frame. NOT pitching. Set the agenda: "I want to understand your situation first, and then if it makes sense I'll show you what we do."

### Phase 2: Context (2-8 min)
Background gathering. "What brought you here today?" Archetype detection starts here based on early signals.

### Phase 3: Problem Development (8-20 min)
The heart of discovery. Go 4-5 levels deep — DO NOT skip ahead:

- **Level 1 — Surface**: What they say first. A broad label. Don't stop here.
- **Level 2 — Probe**: Make it specific. Get numbers. "How many? How long? What exactly?"
- **Level 3 — Duration**: "How long has this been going on?" Builds urgency without manufacturing it.
- **Level 4 — Impact/Emotion**: "What does that do to you emotionally?" TRIGGER WORDS to develop: frustrated, scared, stuck, stressed, pissed off, hard, tough — DO NOT move on when you hear these. Go deeper.
- **Level 5 — Ramifications**: "What if nothing changes in 6 months?" Their words become your close ammo.

### Phase 4: Baseline Assessment (20-25 min)
Skill level 1-10 on relevant domain skills. Low scores are normal, not a barrier.

### Phase 5: Solution Vision (25-30 min)
Bring them UP emotionally. "What does achieving [outcome] look like?"

### Phase 6: Consequence of Inaction (30-33 min)
Bring back DOWN. "What happens if nothing changes over the next 12 months?"

### Phase 7: Blocker Surfacing (33-36 min)
Surface objections BEFORE the pitch. If spouse mentioned — get them on the call NOW.

### Phase 8: Responsibility (36-38 min)
They name why they can't do it alone. "Can't do it alone / would take too long / want a proven process."

### Phase 9: NOW Tie-Down (38-40 min)
"Is this a NOW thing? Scale of 1-10?" Gate before pitch. Below 6 — resolve the blocker first.

### Phase 10: Bridge to Pitch (40-43 min)
Mirror their EXACT words. Name the gap. "That's not a [luck] problem — it's a [root cause] problem."

### Phase 11: Pitch (43-53 min)
Present 4-6 components. After presenting — SHUT UP. Let them ask.

### Phase 12: Temp Check (53-55 min)
1-10 gut check BEFORE price. "What's keeping you from a 9?" Surface the hidden objection.

### Phase 13: Close (55 min+)
State investment clearly. Present payment options. Then SILENCE. DO NOT fill the gap.

## Buyer Archetypes

The Hardly Selling framework identifies five buyer archetypes. Detection begins within the first 5-10 minutes based on language signals.

### Pain Buyer (most common — ~49% close rate)
**Signals**: Laid off/unemployed, scared, applying for months, bills, family depending, "stuck", "washing out", "can't keep going like this"
**Drive**: Escape from pain — they NEED a solution NOW
**Lead with**: Speed and structure. Payment plan upfront (financial objection is common).
**AVOID**: Vision questions ("where do you see yourself in 5 years?"), consequence stacking beyond what's needed.

### Vision Buyer (~50% close rate)
**Signals**: Currently employed, stable income, "want to level up", calm professional tone, references 2-5 year goals, already researching
**Drive**: Identity/aspiration — they want to GROW, not escape
**Lead with**: Destination and identity. Don't manufacture urgency.
**AVOID**: Aggressive urgency pressure, consequence stacking. Longer discovery = higher close rate.

### Crisis Buyer
**Signals**: Pain language PLUS mentions cost/savings/payment plan in first 3 minutes
**Drive**: Affordable path to relief
**Lead with**: Qualify finances BEFORE going deep on pain. Ethical DQ if truly can't afford.

### Convenience Buyer
**Signals**: Asks practical questions early, knows what they want, business-like, no emotional distress
**Drive**: Efficiency and clarity
**Lead with**: Clean options. Skip deep pain excavation. Give them what they need to decide.

### Tire Kicker
**Signals**: Vague answers, "just researching", "no rush", urgency 1-4/10
**Drive**: None this call
**Action**: DQ or BAMFAM by minute 8-10. Do NOT pitch. 0% close rate empirically.

## Universal Principles

These are the core operating principles of the Hardly Selling framework:

1. **Discovery wins**: 80% of closes happen in discovery. Rush it = throwing a Hail Mary at the close.
2. **SUGGEST, don't commit**: "Based on what I've seen, it might be X — does that resonate?" Let THEM confirm.
3. **Lean into the negative**: Don't sidestep fear. "It sounds like you're worried this might not work. Let's talk about that."
4. **Call rhythm**: EVEN (open) → DOWN (problem) → UP (vision) → DOWN (consequence) → close
5. **Three Phases of Confidence**: Discovery = YOU get confident. Pitch = THEY get confident. Close = negotiation.
6. **Fact-check objections**: "Do you truly need to [objection], or are you nervous and pulling that card? Be honest with me."
7. **Ask more than you tell**: The less you talk in discovery, the closer they are to a yes.

## Close Signals

### Triggers — move to price reveal IMMEDIATELY when you hear:
- "When I start" / "when I join" language (not "if")
- Asks about payment options or next steps unprompted
- Rates fit 8-10 unprompted
- Asks detailed follow-up questions about curriculum or logistics

### Danger Signals:
- **"Let me think about it"** → Tie-down: "You said this was a NOW thing. What changed in the last 5 minutes?"
- **Spouse mentioned at close for FIRST TIME** → "Let's get them on a quick 3-minute call right now." NEVER "go talk to them."
- **Silence after price reveal** → DO NOT fill it. Let them think.
- **Urgency 1-4 with no compelling reason** → DQ or BAMFAM. Do NOT pitch.

## Objection Handling

### Core Principle: Use Their Own Words
The most powerful objection handling tool is the prospect's own language. Quote them back to themselves to create productive tension.

### Framework:
1. **Fact-check first**: "Do you truly need to [objection], or are you nervous and pulling that card? Be honest with me."
2. **Identify real vs smokescreen**: If real — honor it. If smokescreen — handle.

### Common Objections:

**"Think About It"**:
- "You already came here because you wanted [goal]. Has that changed?"
- "Usually when people say 'think about it' there's something specific. What is it?"
- Tie-down: "You said this was a NOW thing. What changed in the last 5 minutes?"

**"Need to check with spouse/partner"**:
- "If they were here right now — would they want you to fix this?"
- "Let's get them on a quick 3-minute call right now."
- NEVER say: "Go talk to them and call me back." This kills the close.

**"Too expensive / Can't afford"**:
- "Which part of what I outlined would you like me to remove?"
- "If it was free — would this be the solution for you?"
- Move immediately to payment plan options.

**"Need to research"**:
- "What specifically would your research tell you that would help you decide?"
- "Are you looking for a reason to do this, or a reason NOT to?"

## Five C's Tie-Down

Verify each is in place before closing:

1. **Certainty**: They acknowledged the problem is real
2. **Clarity**: They named what needs to change
3. **Conviction**: They expressed they need to do something
4. **Confidence**: They rated the program fit 7+ after the pitch
5. **Commitment**: They said this is a NOW thing (tie-down phase)
