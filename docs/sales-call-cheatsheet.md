# Consultant Exit - Call Cheat Sheet
## Your Call Flow (45 min)

---

### PHASE 1: INTRO (3-5 min)
- "How's your day going?"
- "Looks like you booked about help landing a role outside consulting?"
- "What are you looking to get out of this call today?"
- Frame: "I'll diagnose what's happening. If we can help, I'll show you how. If not, I'll point you somewhere useful. Sound fair?"

---

### PHASE 2-3: DISCOVERY (15-20 min) — THE WHOLE GAME

Key questions in order:
1. Confirm background (light): "You're at [Firm], [Practice], [Level] — accurate?"
2. "How long have you been feeling this burnout?"
3. "When you try to job search after hours, what actually happens?"
4. "Walk me through your current approach"
5. "Do you actually LIKE doing it this way?"
6. SILENCE — let them say no, don't rescue
7. "If nothing changes in 6 months, what happens?" ← THE MONEY QUESTION
8. "What does success look like 12 months from now?"

**RULE: After every pain question, count to 5 silently. Do not fill the gap.**

---

### PHASE 4: COMMITMENT BRIDGE (5 min) — BEFORE PRICE

- "Are you willing to settle for [their status quo]?" → Wait for No
- "Why now? You haven't changed for [X] months." → Let them convince themselves
- "Whose responsibility is it to fix this?" → They say "Mine"

**If you skip this, every objection = money. If you nail it, money = secondary.**

---

### PHASE 5: ASSESSMENT (5 min) — Pull up Airtable

- "Let me show you what our recruiting team put together."
- "They flagged you as a strong fit for [Role Families]"
- "Timeline: [X-Y days] to get in front of hiring managers"
- "They noted [specific strength]"
- "Does that align with what you're hoping for?"
- "Are you locked in on [role family] or still working through that?"

---

### PHASE 6: PRESCRIPTION (5-7 min) — Three Pillars

**Pillar 1 - Positioning:** "Instead of throwing apps everywhere, you know exactly what you're going after"
- "Does that make sense?" (pause)

**Pillar 2 - Execution:** "10-15 roles/week, daily outreach, consistent interview flow"
- "Does that make sense?" (pause)

**Pillar 3 - Exit Skill for Life:** "You leave with the ability to never feel trapped again"
- "Does that make sense?" (pause)

"Does this feel like what you've been looking for?" → "Why though?"

---

### PHASE 7: PRICE (2 min) — State it, shut up

"Investment is 6% of base salary. $6K upfront applied toward total. Rest due at placement."

**SILENCE. First person to speak loses.**

"How would you like to proceed?"

Example: $200K role = $12K total. $6K paid, $6K at placement.
Example: $150K role = $9K total. $6K paid, $3K at placement.

---

### PHASE 8: OBJECTIONS

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Need to think about it" | "What specifically?" → "You said it's your responsibility. What changed?" |
| "Too expensive" | "Is it the total or the $6K upfront?" → Klarna / $500 deposit |
| "Talk to spouse" | "What will they say?" → Book 15 min tomorrow |
| "Try on my own" | "You've been at it [X months]. What would be different?" |
| "Send me info" | "What specifically haven't we covered?" → Book 15 min follow-up |

### CLOSE LEVERS
- **$500 deposit** → Meet recruiter, refundable if not a fit
- **Same-day close** → $5K instead of $6K (today only)
- **Klarna** → Monthly payments on upfront fee
- **$12K cap** → Only if assessment score 8+ and role >$200K

---

### RED FLAGS — End the call politely
- Can't explain why they want to leave
- Won't share salary/goals
- Wants free advice
- No budget, no willingness to invest
- "Just exploring" with zero timeline

---

### THE 3 MOMENTS THAT WIN OR LOSE
1. **Discovery silence** — Let their pain breathe
2. **Commitment bridge** — "Whose responsibility?" before price
3. **Price silence** — State it, stop talking
